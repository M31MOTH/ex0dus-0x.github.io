[hack] introductions

[hack] (or bracket-hack-bracket, or hack array) is a collaborative group that I am creating. It is NOT a hacker group, but just a group for like-minded hacktivists who wish to share their knowledge and improve their skills and competency.

[hack] will also be a sort-of class, where people who wish to learn more about security, programming, computer science, and etc can come together and learn more through community-driven courses and lessons. Assignments are given, but optional to complete.

Just like NYC Resistor, and many other makerspaces and hackerspaces, this group fosters creativity. [hack] has been set up as an organization on GitHub, and I am looking forward to the member's ideas and projects, which I want to push as a repository.

[hack] rules and regulations

  • 1. Please don't threaten other members, especially if you are uncapable to do anything yourself
  • 2. You do not need to have any experience in code or security, but please have basic computer knowledge
  • 3. Always help your fellow members. They are just as valued as you
  • 4. I encourage passive arguing and debate. I love it when people argue over controversial topics, since its exciting to look at different perspectives
  • 5. Don't worry about inactivity. I probably will be inactive sometimes. This group is sort of like membership to a club. You don't necessarily have to be around all the time
  • 6. Always have the capacity and competency to learn!
